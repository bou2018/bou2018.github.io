Health Care Basics

Health Insurance

Covers the cost of medical care, started in 1920s

Key Players

Payer - provides a health insurance

Member - avail a health insurance from the payer

Providers - provide medical/clinical services

Cost Sharing

Deductible - amount to pay before the insurance starts to pay

Copay - fee paid each time you see a doctor or fill a prescription

Coinsurance - money to pay after you have paid deductible

Indemnity Plans and MCO

Indemnity - health plan that do not use deductible or copay

Managed Care - delivering comprehensive care services

CDHP

CDHP - Consumer Directed Health Plan

FSA - oldest type of CDHP account

Medicare

Administered by CMS

For people aged 65 or above and people with disability

Medicaid

For people with low income rate who cannot afford healthcare

Main Group accounts for Medicaid is mainly for Elderly and disabled adults

Medicare Choices

Medicare Part A -Inpatient coverage -Covers hospital services

Medicare Part B -Outpatient coverage -Covers Physician’s professional services

Medicare Part C -Medicare Advantage -Provides Part A, Part B and sometimes even Part D coverage

Medicare Part D -Drug Coverage -Introduced in 2006-Covers the prescribed drugs

Tricare

Covers military personel, military retirees

Managed by the Defense Health Agency (DHA)

Individual and Family plans

An individual plan is taken by one person

A family plan is for two or more members

Group plans

Small group plans premium must on benefits and quality improvement

Large group plans premium dollars collected by insurance companies

Premium depends on prior claim history
